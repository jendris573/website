Research

My current research focus is on the effects of climate change on forest communities. I specifically investigate the effects of warming winters on the spring leaf-out timeframe to examine the risks associated with shifting leaf-out times compared to last freeze dates. This research is focused on three deciduous hardwood species: Liriodendron tulipifera, Fagus grandifolia, and Acer saccharum.

Additionally I am interested in the effects of extreme heat during the summer growing season. Specifically I examine the thermal safety margins between extreme air temperatures and leaf temperatures against the thermal tolerances of 11 species of deciduous trees to gain a community level understanding of risks from extreme heat.

And to examine if the thermal safety margins are a conserved trait, I have conducted the research along a latitudinal gradient. This spans between Birmingham, AL to Bloomington, IN for the spring research and between Birmingham, AL to Ann Arbor, MI for the summer work.
